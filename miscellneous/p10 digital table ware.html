DIGITAL TABLEWARE

Programmable dishes , bowl and cutlery

Date

1 Dec. 2017

Category

Nanofabrication, Interaction Design

Contributers

Purnendu, Kristian Gohlke,
Prof. Eva Hornecker

Digital Tableware is a concept proposing programmable shape changing tableware as user interfaces. Here we are proposing multiple interactions considering Tableware as a tangible interface.

One major part of the interaction is a shape changing dish-bowl which has the ability to sense the type of food and hence morph its shape accordingly. The shape change is facilitated by Origami based mechanism.

This was the major part of the work I pursued at the Bauhaus University Weimer working under supervision of Prof. Eva Hornecker in the summer of 2017.
